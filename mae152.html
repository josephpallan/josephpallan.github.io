MAE 152: Introduction to Manual and CNC machining

As a mechanical engineer, this is the first opportunity to combine
design and engineering into realising a part. This class allowed me to
understand the feasibility of my own ideas and increased my physical awareness.

Manual Mill and Lathe

Using manual machines helped me realise the principles behind good machining practices. Furthermore, creating machined surfaces that are flat, centering around holes, and calibrating offsets for tooling showed me the techniques needed to make parts as designed and the impact of tolerances.

CNC Mills and Lathe using CAM

Using knowledge from manual milling, we are able to use computer-aided-manufacturing (CAM) software such as Fusion 360 to create toolpaths given a specific stock dimension. This was combined with CNC mills and lathes to speed up production, however, it required similar knowledge of machinist knowledges such as feed rates, tooling offsets, and layer height controls.

The next step in this process is to learn 4D CNC methods to enable greater complexity in designing parts, and less constraints about fixturing and zeroing.

Waterjet, Bandsaw, Laser-cutting, and other equipment.

Making 2D cuts on flat sheets of metal, plastics, foams, using these tools allows for rapid prototyping, and enabled me to generate layer-by-layer designs.
