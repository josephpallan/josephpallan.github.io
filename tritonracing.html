Triton Racing

Triton Racing is UC San Diego's Formula SAE team.
We design, manufacture, assemble, and validate a race car which
competes with 50 other universities across the U.S.

Aerodynamic Devices Design

Using Solidworks, Spaceclaim, and other geometry defining software, we figure out the plausible cases defined by the rules governing our design intent. From this, I designed a model to accuratly depict effects from bodywork, and suspension components affecting wake around aerodynamic devices.

Simulation

The model is then put in CFD software (Ansys Fluent, Solidworks integrated CFD, etc.) to parametrically study changes such as pitch, roll, and yaw on the performance of components. This process is repetitive as designs change based on results from simulation. While real-world, and wind tunnel testing using smaller models would be ideal, this is the best compromise of cost and time for our team.

Manufacturing Carbon Fibre Composites

The designs are converted to male and female molds. These are made using CNC milling machines. Then carbon fiber is laid up on top of this surface with epoxy (or similar polymer matrix composite). The entire part is put under vacuum to remove air pockets, and consolidate the layers of fibre.

Validating Performance

As a low-budget team, we use flow-visualising paint over specific regions of our car to see how it streaks across a surface as it dries. Furthermore, small pieces of yarn are used as Tufts to photograph and compare flow structures in different points across a performance interval (e.g. Accelerating, Braking, Turning).

Overall Project

As a team of 15-20 undegraduate students, we spend an enoromous amount of time into this hobby. Each year, a new set of students come into this organsiation. Even if our team has one brilliant year, it could be a completely different story the next year depending on how well we're able to delegate, project manage, and handle all the obstacles thrown at us. For 2023-2024, our team is switching to EV to embrace change and the sustainable goals it has.
