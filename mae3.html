MAE 3: Introduction to Engineering Design and Graphics

My first engineering-design focused class at UCSD.

Weight-driven clock using the small angle approximation

Designing a weight-driven clock with the goal of understanding simple engineering priciples (e.g. point mass model, small angle approximation, natural frequency)

Using 2D contours, and vectorized graphics, I was able to outline specific shapes in CAD software to meet the design requirements and calculate theoretical timing values.

The pitfalls and successes of the experiment was highlights in the results and discussion section, with potential sources of error.

MATLAB plots

In order to visually detect aliasing, and directly compare signals sampled at different rates, MATLAB plots were scripted to indicate noise and other phenomena.

This GIF made using MATLAB contour plots shows a sound wave propagating through a field of 300mm by 150mm where each frame corresponds corresponds to each data point captured to show the full spatiotemporal sound field generated by a speaker.

Designing and manufacturing a robot

Using design and fabrication skills from previous projects, I designed the gearbox and motor mounting mechanisms for our final project.

Our team fought off two robots before being eliminated due to the timing belt slipping off. Unfortunatly, this failure was recognised beforehand, but without access to workspaces during the last portion of the class, we were unable to implement any type of passive belt tensioner.
